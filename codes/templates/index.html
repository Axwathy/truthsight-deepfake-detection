FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our deepfake detection service.

A deepfake is synthetic media created using artificial intelligence, typically involving the replacement of a person's face or voice in a video with someone else's likeness. The term combines "deep learning" and "fake." These can range from harmless entertainment to malicious misinformation.

Our system achieves approximately 99.2% accuracy on standard deepfake datasets. However, detection accuracy can vary depending on video quality, compression, and the sophistication of the deepfake generation method. We continuously update our models to detect newer manipulation techniques.

We support all major video formats including MP4, AVI, MOV, MKV, and WEBM. The maximum file size is 500MB. If your video is in a different format, we recommend converting it to MP4 before uploading.

Absolutely. Your privacy is our top priority. All videos are processed securely and automatically deleted immediately after analysis. We do not store, share, or use your videos for any purpose other than the requested analysis. No human ever views your uploaded content.

Analysis time depends on video length and resolution. Most videos are processed within 30 seconds to 2 minutes. Longer videos or those with many faces may take additional time. Our system samples one frame per second to balance speed and accuracy.

Our deepfake detection specifically analyzes facial features to identify manipulation. If no faces are detected in the video, we cannot provide a classification. Ensure your video contains clearly visible faces. Very small faces, extreme angles, or heavy occlusion may not be detected.

Our system is optimized for detecting face-swap deepfakes, which are the most common type. It may be less effective against other manipulation types like voice cloning, full-body puppeteering, or non-facial video manipulations. We're continuously expanding our detection capabilities.
